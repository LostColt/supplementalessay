Research

Before you can write a compelling "Why This School" essay, you need to know specific details about the school. This guide shows you exactly how to research effectively in 20-30 minutes per school.

When to use this guide: Only for "Why This School" or "Why This Major" essays. Other essay types don't require school research.

Your Goal

You're not trying to become an expert on the entire university. You're collecting 4-5 specific details (programs, professors, courses) to show you've done your homework and understand what makes this school unique.

Don't just Google "Why is Northwestern good?" - you need specific programs, professors, courses, and opportunities that connect to your interests.

The difference: Specificity. Names, numbers, program titles, course codes.

Secondary Research (If Schedule Permits)

Don't delay your core research waiting for these, but if they are available, use them.

Virtual Events & Rep Visits: If a rep visits your high school or there is a virtual info session, attend it.

The Strategy: Do your core research (Sources 1-4) first. Then, use these sessions to ask specific questions about what you found (e.g., "I saw Professor Chen is researching CRISPR...").
