Impact Experience

Here's the thing about leadership essays: schools don't just want to know that you were captain or president of something. They want to see that you actually made something happen.

Remember: Titles don't matter. Impact does. Did you notice a problem? Did you do something about it?

Impact can be small and still matter. You noticed your robotics team had terrible communication, so you started a shared system that everyone actually used. You saw your school's recycling program was a mess, so you reorganized it.

Show Your Leadership Style

Different people lead in different ways. Some lead by asking good questions. Some lead by doing the work nobody else wants to do. The essay should show YOUR style.

How to Open Strong

❌ The Snooze Button (Generic)

"I am a natural leader and have always enjoyed taking charge of group projects."

✅ The Hook (Specific)

"Three weeks into freshman year, I realized our debate team had a problem. The seniors ran every practice, chose every topic, and made every decision. The underclassmen just sat there."

Don't Skip the Hard Parts

The best leadership essays show the actual process-the resistance you got, the mistakes you made, the adjustments you had to make along the way.

❌ Surface Level

"I organized a school fundraiser and we raised $2,000 for charity. It was very successful."

✅ Shows Real Leadership

"I proposed a school fundraiser, but the student council said no one would show up. So I spent a week surveying classes... brought back data... and got approval. Then our lead performer dropped out two days before the event... I had to convince three nervous freshmen that their poetry slam act was exactly what we needed. We raised $2,000, but more importantly, those freshmen kept performing all year."
