The Writing
Workshop

You've done your research. You've chosen your essay strategy. Now, use this workshop to draft and polish your essay.

For juniors: This process works whether you're writing your first supplemental or your tenth. Come back to this page every time you are ready to draft.

The Workflow Checklist

Use this as a checklist. Check off each step as you complete it.

Step 1: Choose Your Structure

Essay Length Intro Strategy Body Strategy Conclusion Strategy
Ultra-Short (50-100 words) Skip it. Dive straight into substance. Every word delivers content. End with your strongest forward-looking point.
Short (150-250 words) 1 Sentence. A brief hook. 4-5 sentences of substance. 1 Sentence. A brief closer.
Medium (250-400 words) 2-3 Sentences. Set the context. The heart of the essay. 1-2 Sentences. Wrap up clearly.
Long (500+ words) Full Paragraph. Traditional hook & thesis. 1-2 developed paragraphs. Full Paragraph. Traditional conclusion.

Write down: My essay is ___ words, so I'll use the ___ structure.

Step 2: Draft Using These Core Principles (30-60 minutes)

Now you're ready to write. Keep these principles in mind:

Principle 1: Start Late, Grab Early

Don't waste words on setup. Get to the heart of your story within the first 1-2 sentences.

Principle 2: Show, Don't Tell

Don't just state qualities ("I am a hard worker"). Demonstrate them through specific actions ("I spent three weeks troubleshooting a failed experiment").

Principle 3: The Coffee Shop Test

Write like you talk. If you wouldn't say "There are several reasons why I am interested in attending" to a friend in a coffee shop, don't write it in your essay.

Principle 4: Apply the "So What?" Test

After every major point, ask: "So what? Why does this matter?" Consider: Personal Growth, Skill Development, Intellectual Insight, Future Application.

Mechanism: Use a Bridge Phrase to connect the story to the insight. Try starting a sentence with: "This experience taught me that..." or "I realized that..."

Now write your first draft. Don't worry about perfection-just get words on the page.

Step 3: Revise Using These Three Tests (20-30 minutes)

You have a draft. Now make it better. Run your essay through these three tests:

Test 1: The One-Breath Test

Read each sentence aloud in one breath. If you can't finish it comfortably, it's too long.

Test 2: The Deletion Test

Remove each sentence one at a time. If the essay still makes sense without it, delete it.

Test 3: The Specificity Test

Look for vague statements ("strong alumni network") and replace them with concrete details ("The mentorship program in the Media Lab").

Step 4: The Red Flag Check (The Editor's Hat)

Before you share your essay, check for these common mistakes based on your essay type.

Why This School
Did you just compliment the school ("Your campus is beautiful")?
Did you mention a professor without connecting their work to your interests?
Did you forget to show what you have already done in this field?
Community
Did you describe the community but forget to explain your role in it?
Did you list too many communities instead of going deep on one?
Did you focus only on what you got, not what you gave?
Meaningful Activity
Did you just describe what you do without reflecting on why it matters?
Did you make it sound like you never struggled? (Show the hard parts!)
Did you force a connection to your major that isn't really there?
Leadership
Did you focus on your title instead of your actions?
Did you take all the credit and ignore the team?
Did you pretend everything went perfectly? (Real leadership is messy).
Intellectual Passion
Did you just summarize facts instead of showing your curiosity?
Did you choose a topic just to sound smart?
Did you claim to have it all figured out? (Admit what you don't know yet).

Step 5: Get Feedback (10 minutes to request + 24-48 hours wait time)

Share your essay with your college counselor via Google Docs. Ask: "Does this sound like me?"

Quick Troubleshooting

"I'm staring at a blank page and can't start."

Answer: Skip the intro. Write the 'Middle' first (the story of what you actually did).
